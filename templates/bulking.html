Bulking Plan

Bulking is a planned process where a person eats extra food and follows proper exercise to gain muscle mass and body weight. During bulking, you should eat around 300–500 extra calories per day more than your normal need. These calories should come from healthy foods, not junk. Protein intake should be high, around 1.6–2.2 grams per kilogram of body weight, because protein helps in building and repairing muscles. Carbohydrates should make up a large part of the diet, as they provide energy for workouts and help muscles recover. Healthy fats should also be included in moderate amounts to support hormones and overall health. Meals should be spread throughout the day, ideally 4–6 meals, and every meal should include some protein. Along with eating more, exercise is very important. Strength training should be done 4–5 days a week, focusing on compound exercises like squats, bench press, deadlifts, rows, and shoulder press. Each muscle group should be trained properly, with enough weight to challenge the muscles. Progressive overload, which means slowly increasing weight or reps, is necessary for muscle growth. Rest and sleep are equally important; at least 7–9 hours of sleep is needed for muscle recovery. Drinking enough water helps digestion, performance, and recovery. Avoid skipping meals and avoid too much junk food. Bulking should be done slowly and consistently to gain more muscle and less fat.

Calorie Surplus
  • Eat 300–500 extra calories daily for muscle gain
  • Calories give energy for workouts and recovery
  • Too few calories = no muscle growth
Protein (Most Important Macro)
  • Aim for 1.6–2.2 g per kg body weight
  • Needed after workouts for recovery
  • Sources: eggs, milk, chicken, fish, beans, lentils
Carbohydrates
  • Make up 45–60% of total calories
  • Fuel workouts and help muscle growth
  • Sources: rice, oats, whole grains, fruits, vegetables
Fats
  • 20–30% of total calories
  • Supports hormones and overall health
  • Sources: nuts, seeds, olive oil, fatty fish

Bulking Diet Meal Plan

🥣 Breakfast
  • 4 whole eggs
  • 2 slices whole wheat bread
  • 1 glass milk
  • 1 banana or apple
🥗 Lunch
  • 1–2 cups cooked rice
  • 150–200g chicken / paneer / dal
  • Mixed vegetables
  • 1 bowl curd
🍲 Snacks
  • Handful of nuts (almonds, peanuts)
  • Fruit smoothie or milk shake
  • Peanut butter sandwich
🥜 Pre-Workout Meal
  • 1 banana or oats
  • 1 scoop whey protein (optional)
  • Black coffee (optional)
🍛 Dinner
  • 2 chapatis or rice
  • 150–200g fish / chicken / paneer
  • Vegetable curry or salad
